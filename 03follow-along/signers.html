Who were the signers

Popular Signers

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence, third President of the United States, remembered for the Louisiana Purchase and founding the University of Virginia.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence in Congress, second President of the United States, and crucial in convincing others to support the Declaration.
Benjamin Franklin
World-famous scientist, inventor, and diplomat; secured French support during the Revolutionary War; celebrated for his wit and reputation.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress at the time of signing; known for his bold, oversized signature; one of the wealthiest men in the colonies and a key patriot leader.
Samuel Adams
Leading organizer of colonial resistance including the Boston Tea Party; master of revolutionary propaganda; later served as Governor of Massachusetts.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution for independence in Congress; strong supporter of liberty and state rights.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration; known for his wealth and political influence; the last surviving signer, living until 1832.
